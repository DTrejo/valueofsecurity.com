# Thinking frameworks  
  
To ensure you have a complete master reference, here is the unified list of every framework we have discussed, categorized by their "superpower," along with their definitive "Bibles."  
### 1. Flow & Operational Efficiency  
*These frameworks help you understand how value moves through a system and where it gets stuck.*  
 * **Value Stream Mapping (VSM)**  
   * **The Book:** *Learning to See* by **Mike Rother and John Shook**.  
   * **The Gist:** Visualizing the flow of material and information to eliminate waste (Muda).  
 * **Theory of Constraints (TOC)**  
   * **The Book:** *The Goal* by **Eliyahu M. Goldratt**.  
   * **The Gist:** Identifying the single "bottleneck" that limits the throughput of the entire system.  
 * **The Flow Framework**  
   * **The Book:** *Project to Product* by **Mik Kersten**.  
   * **The Gist:** A bridge between business strategy and technology delivery for the digital age.  
### 2. Strategy & Market Positioning  
*These frameworks help you map the external landscape and choose where to fight.*  
 * **Wardley Mapping**  
   * **The Book:** *Wardley Maps* by **Simon Wardley**.  
   * **The Gist:** Mapping components by evolution and visibility to anticipate market shifts.  
 * **The Five Forces**  
   * **The Book:** *Competitive Strategy* by **Michael Porter**.  
   * **The Gist:** Analyzing industry attractiveness based on buyers, suppliers, substitutes, and rivals.  
 * **Blue Ocean Strategy**  
   * **The Book:** *Blue Ocean Strategy* by **W. Chan Kim and Renée Mauborgne**.  
   * **The Gist:** Creating uncontested market space rather than competing in "bloody" red oceans.  
 * **Crossing the Chasm**  
   * **The Book:** *Crossing the Chasm* by **Geoffrey Moore**.  
   * **The Gist:** How to move high-tech products from early adopters to the mainstream market.  
 * **The Three Horizons of Innovation**  
   * **The Book:** *The Alchemy of Growth* by **Mehrdad Baghai et al.**  
   * **The Gist:** Balancing current core business with future visionary bets.  
### 3. Complexity & Systems Thinking  
*These frameworks help you understand deep-seated patterns and non-linear logic.*  
 * **Systems Thinking**  
   * **The Book:** *Thinking in Systems: A Primer* by **Donella Meadows**.  
   * **The Gist:** Understanding feedback loops, stocks, flows, and leverage points.  
 * **The Cynefin Framework**  
   * **The Book:** *Cynefin - Weaving Sense-Making into the Fabric of Our World* by **Dave Snowden**.  
   * **The Gist:** Categorizing problems into domains (Clear, Complicated, Complex, Chaotic) to dictate the right response.  
 * **Viable System Model (VSM)**  
   * **The Book:** *Brain of the Firm* by **Stafford Beer**.  
   * **The Gist:** Organizational cybernetics—designing a system capable of independent existence.  
 * **Holistic Management**  
   * **The Book:** *Holistic Management* by **Allan Savory**.  
   * **The Gist:** A decision-making framework that tests every action against a social, environmental, and financial "holistic context."  
### 4. Design & Customer Experience  
*These frameworks focus on the "human" element of the business value chain.*  
 * **Service Blueprinting**  
   * **The Book:** *Service Design: From Insight to Implementation* by **Andy Polaine et al.**  
   * **The Gist:** Mapping the customer journey alongside the "back-stage" processes that support it.  
 * **Jobs to Be Done (JTBD)**  
   * **The Book:** *Competing Against Luck* by **Clayton Christensen**.  
   * **The Gist:** Understanding the underlying "job" a customer is trying to accomplish.  
 * **Design Thinking**  
   * **The Book:** *Change by Design* by **Tim Brown**.  
   * **The Gist:** Using empathy, ideation, and prototyping to solve human-centered problems.  
 * **The Business Model Canvas**  
   * **The Book:** *Business Model Generation* by **Alexander Osterwalder**.  
   * **The Gist:** A one-page visual map of how a business creates, delivers, and captures value.  
### 5. Management & Governance  
*These frameworks focus on how people are organized and how decisions are made.*  
 * **The McKinsey 7S Framework**  
   * **The Book:** *In Search of Excellence* by **Tom Peters and Robert Waterman**.  
   * **The Gist:** Aligning seven internal elements (Staff, Skills, Style, etc.) to ensure organizational success.  
 * **Objectives and Key Results (OKRs)**  
   * **The Book:** *Measure What Matters* by **John Doerr**.  
   * **The Gist:** Aligning team goals with measurable, high-impact outcomes.  
 * **Holacracy**  
   * **The Book:** *Holacracy* by **Brian Robertson**.  
   * **The Gist:** Distributed authority and self-management via circles and roles.  
 * **Situational Leadership**  
   * **The Book:** *Management of Organizational Behavior* by **Hersey and Blanchard**.  
   * **The Gist:** Adapting leadership style (Directing to Delegating) based on a team member's maturity.  
 * **The Five Dysfunctions of a Team**  
   * **The Book:** *The Five Dysfunctions of a Team* by **Patrick Lencioni**.  
   * **The Gist:** A pyramid model for building trust and accountability in groups.  
 * **Spiral Dynamics**  
   * **The Book:** *Spiral Dynamics* by **Don Beck and Christopher Cowan**.  
   * **The Gist:** Understanding the evolution of human value systems and cultural worldviews.  
### 6. Cognitive & Decision Science  
*These frameworks help you "think about thinking."*  
 * **Dual Process Theory**  
   * **The Book:** *Thinking, Fast and Slow* by **Daniel Kahneman**.  
   * **The Gist:** Recognizing the difference between System 1 (instinct) and System 2 (logic).  
 * **Probabilistic Thinking**  
   * **The Book:** *Thinking in Bets* by **Annie Duke**.  
   * **The Gist:** Embracing uncertainty and separating the quality of a decision from its outcome.  
 * **The OODA Loop**  
   * **The Book:** *Certain to Win* by **Chet Richards**.  
   * **The Gist:** Observe, Orient, Decide, Act—the cycle of rapid decision-making in competitive environments.  
 * **First Principles Thinking**  
   * **The Book:** *Principles* by **Ray Dalio**.  
   * **The Gist:** Deconstructing problems to their most basic truths and building up from there.
