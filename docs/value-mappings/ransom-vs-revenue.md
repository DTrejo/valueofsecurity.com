Recent data from 2024 through early 2026 shows that ransomware groups have moved from "spray and pray" tactics to a highly calculated, revenue-based pricing model. Demands are typically calibrated to represent **0.5% to 5% of annual revenue**, depending on the critical nature of the industry.

## **Ransom Demands by Annual Revenue (2025–2026 Data)**

## **Recent Famous Examples (2024–2026)**

### **1. The "Big Game" Payout: Undisclosed Fortune 50 Company (2024)**
* **Revenue:** Estimated **$100B+** * **The Ransom:** **$75 Million**
* **The Story:** This is currently cited as the largest single confirmed ransomware payment in history. The company was targeted by the **Dark Angels** group. The payout reflects the extreme end of the spectrum for companies where even 24 hours of downtime results in billions in lost productivity.

### **2. The Healthcare Stoppage: Change Healthcare (2024)**
* **Revenue:** Part of UnitedHealth Group (**$370B+** annual revenue)
* **The Ransom:** **$22 Million**
* **The Story:** A classic example of "complete business stoppage." Because Change Healthcare processed 1 in 3 US patient records, the leverage was absolute. They paid $22M to the **ALPHV/BlackCat** group just to get the keys to resume medical billing and pharmacy services across the country.

### **3. The Public Sector Squeeze: City of Columbus (2024)**
* **Revenue (Budget):** Approx **$1.1 Billion**
* **The Demand:** **$1.8 Million** (Rhysida Group)
* **The Story:** Unlike corporations, cities often refuse to pay due to political pressure. Columbus refused, leading to the dumping of 6.5TB of data. This highlights a trend: public entities with revenue (budgets) in the $1B range are often hit with demands in the **$1M–$2M** sweet spot.

### **4. The Specialized Mid-Market: Synnovis (2024)**
* **Revenue:** Mid-market healthcare provider.
* **The Impact:** Total stoppage of pathology services for the UK NHS.
* **The Story:** Targeted by the **Qilin** group. While the exact payout remains debated, the recovery cost to the UK health system was estimated in the tens of millions due to the specialized nature of the business stoppage.

## **Key Trend: The "Negotiation Gap"**
Research in late 2025 indicates that **53% of companies** now successfully negotiate the initial demand down. 
* **Large Cap ($1B+):** Have the most leverage to negotiate because they often have sophisticated "Ransomware Negotiators" on retainer through their insurance.
* **Small Cap (<$50M):** Usually have the least leverage and often pay closer to the original demand because they lack the backups to walk away.

**Note on "Extortion-Only" vs. "Stoppage":** In 2026, we are seeing a pivot. Because backups are getting better, attackers are moving away from just "stopping" the business (encryption) and moving toward **exfiltration**, where they threaten to leak data unless paid, regardless of whether the business is currently "stopped."
